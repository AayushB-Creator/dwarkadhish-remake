Shree Dwarkadhish Temple

Shree Dwarkadhish Mandir is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, worshipped here as Dwarkadhish, the 'King of Dwarka'.

Located on the sacred banks of the Gomti River, the temple stands as a symbol of faith, devotion, and timeless heritage. Its majestic five-storied structure, supported by 72 pillars, is believed to be over 2,500 years old, making it one of the oldest and most significant pilgrimage sites in India.

The Mandir is not only a spiritual center but also a vibrant hub of culture and tradition, drawing millions of devotees and tourists from across the world. The daily rituals, grand festivals, and the mesmerizing sound of temple bells create an atmosphere filled with divine energy and peace.
